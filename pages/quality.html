Quality

If there is something that distinguishes us from other translation agencies, that is our quality.

Why Should You Choose Us?

All of our linguists are certified and qualified to translate any material, whether it is medical, legal, technical or financial. To keep consistency in all projects we work on for a certain client, we use termbases and glossaries which we update on a regular basis, as we work on more requests. To develop a termbase or glossary, these are the steps we follow:

  1. Document Review: Close review of documents that have already been translated for the client. If the client is able to provide reference files, we base our glossaries on the terminology used on such documents, paying close attention to how key terms or words used consistently across the texts have been translated. If no reference documents are provided by the client, we base our glossary on our own translations of their texts.
  2. Preparation of the Termbase: We decide exactly which terms should be included and are relevant to the termbase.
  3. Peer Review: Our team of linguists review the glossary and make comments. In case of a disagreement, this is flagged with a comment for further review. Decisions are made based on the research conducted by our linguists.
  4. Review by Client: The termbase is shared with our client for their review. The client (or their reviewers) makes comments and suggestions on how to improve the termbase.
  5. Implementation of Client’s Feedback: Our team of linguists review the client’s comments and implement their suggestions to the termbase accordingly. In case of a disagreement, our linguists insert comments explaining the reason for disagreement.

Our Step-by-Step Process

1. Text review and preparation

The translator carefully reads the text in preparation for the next step and identifies any unknown terminology that will need to be researched. Reference documents, such as similar translated documents, may also be searched during this step.

2. Translation

The actual process of translation begins here. After a close look at the text, the translator converts the text into the other language by conveying the meaning, changing the structure of the sentence to make it sound natural in the target language, taking into account grammar and language usage.

3. Proofreading by second linguist

A second professional linguist reviews the text looking for typos, inaccuracies, grammar, syntax and language mistakes, among other issues that may be found in the translation and corrects them to ensure the text is 100% error-free.

4. Validation of edits

The original translator who worked on the project reviews the edits made by the proofreader and approves or rejects them based on their judgment. Meetings may be held between the translator and proofreader if needed to ensure both linguists are satisfied with the final result.

5. Final review

Our experienced Desktop Publishing team reviews the document against the source language to ensure proper matching of layout and formatting, as well as verify that the whole content has been translated and no parts have been skipped.
